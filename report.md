# DAT.co mNAV Monitor Report

## Selected Indicator

**Indicator:** Modified Net Asset Value (mNAV)

**Why this indicator:**  
mNAV is a standard DAT.co valuation metric because it compares a Bitcoin treasury company's public equity valuation with the USD value of its Bitcoin holdings. It is more informative than a raw premium/discount label because it shows the scale of the market premium directly as a ratio.

## Data Collection

The website combines three inputs:

1. **BTC daily price**
   - Source: Binance BTCUSDT daily klines endpoint
2. **MSTR daily stock price**
   - Source: Nasdaq historical quote endpoint
3. **Strategy Bitcoin holdings**
   - Source: Strategy official `/purchases` page, with `/shares` and press-release fallback

The processed dataset is generated by `scripts/build-dataset.mjs` and stored at `data/processed/mstr-mnav.json`.

## Indicator Definition

```text
mNAV = marketCap / (btcHoldings * btcPrice)
```

Where:

- `marketCap = stockPrice * sharesOutstanding`
- `btcHoldings` is the latest confirmed treasury balance for each date
- `btcPrice` is the Bitcoin USD close used for the same date

## Relationship with Bitcoin (BTC)

Strategy is effectively a leveraged public-market wrapper around a large Bitcoin treasury. Because of this:

- when BTC rises, the treasury NAV rises immediately
- when MSTR rises faster than the implied NAV, mNAV expands
- when BTC rises faster than MSTR, mNAV compresses
- large mNAV premiums may reflect investor appetite for leveraged BTC exposure, capital-markets optionality, management execution, or index/liquidity effects

This makes mNAV a useful lens for understanding whether the equity market is pricing Strategy simply as its Bitcoin holdings or assigning an additional premium above treasury value.
